_(First, before we begin our discussion, I want to mention to our friends and brothers a tradition from the Messenger of God, may God bless him and his family and grant them peace. In that tradition the Messenger of God is reported to have said: «raḥima-llāhu imra'an ʿamila ʿamalan fa-atqanahu»footnote:s24-1[Hadith no. 8. "May God have mercy on a person who performs a deed and does it with perfection."] — that is, may God have mercy on a person who, whatever he does, does it firmly and soundly. Let him do his work solidly; meaning, let him also take account of the possibilities in his work. The possibility that the electricity may go out, the possibility that the microphone may fail, the possibility of dozens of other such incidents. Today's incident became a lesson for us. How good it is for us to think in advance about the things that might happen._

_Our master, the Commander of the Faithful — God's blessings and peace be upon him — expresses this very point in a sermon of the Nahj al-Balāgha. He says: I am not like that stunned, dazed animal that sleeps in its lair until the hunter comes and seizes it; rather, from the events — from what has befallen me and occurred to me — I make use of them for understanding subsequent matters; I gather experience. And this is a lesson for us, that in all the affairs of life we should gather experiences from what has passed for what remains, and take account of the possibilities and the events that may occur.)_

Permit me to speak in summary, although the discussion requires elaboration — and of course I too will elaborate the discussion to the extent that I deem necessary for it to continue; I will continue it for another day or two days, until the matter becomes clear. In brief, what needs to be presented in the continuation of the discussion of Wilayat(((Wilayah))) is this: that an Islamic society and a community formed as the Islamic Ummah(((Ummah))) — meaning a community administered by divine regulations, by a divine mode of thought, and by legislation and the execution of law through divine power — this community, which we name the Islamic Ummah; if this Islamic Ummah wishes to possess Wilayat in that Qur'anic sense which I spoke of yesterday — little by little we arrive at that meaning of Wilayat held in the minds of the elite. That Wilayat which is in the minds of the common people, we are not concerned with now; that Wilayat which is in the minds of the elite — that is, the scholars and the learned and the men of knowledge who are versed in Islamic and Shiʿi sciences — we will come to that now.

But for now the discussion is about Qur'anic Wilayat. If they wish to attain that Qur'anic Wilayat — that Wilayat which is raised in the Qur'an — if the Islamic Ummah wishes to secure it for itself, it must observe two directions. One direction is the direction of internal relations, within the Islamic society; one direction is the direction of external relations — that is, the relation of the Islamic world and the Islamic Ummah and the Islamic society with other communities.@@CX(Wilayah | Qur'anic Wilayat has two directions, internal and external relations)@@

In the sphere of internal relations, the Islamic Ummah possesses Wilayat in the Qur'anic sense only when it secures within itself the utmost solidarity, connection, relationship, and unity of ranks, and the greatest possible cohesion of its various individuals and factions. Let there be no kind of division or disagreement throughout the great Islamic Ummah. Let no separate ranks be formed within this Ummah.@@CX(Ummah | internal Wilayat demands solidarity and unity of ranks with no division)@@

If two units within the Islamic Ummah rise up against one another in war — for instance, suppose a group in the east of the Islamic lands and a group in the northeast of the Islamic lands set out to fight and do battle with one another — the rule is, the Qur'anic command is, that the rest of the Muslims should strive as much as they can to reconcile those two hostile and mutually hostile parties.footnote:s24-2[(kh-ṣ-m) one who shows enmity, [the two parties] showing enmity against one another.] If they see that of these two adversaries one is willing to reconcile, but the other is not willing to reconcile, or the word of that other is a word of coercion while the word of this one is a word of truth — and that man who supports coercion is not willing to submit to the word of truth and to incline toward the just word — in such a situation, the whole Islamic world must become as one, must join hands, must go after that aggressor and silence him. They must contend with him until they set him back in his place.@@CX(Ummah | Muslims must reconcile two warring factions and fight the aggressor who refuses the just word)@@

It is a verse of the Qur'an:

"And if two factions among the believers fight" — if two parties, two groups among the Muslim groups, fight one another; "then make reconciliation between the two" — reconcile between these two Muslim groups. "But if one of them oppresses the other" — if among these two groups one wrongs the other, commits transgression and aggression and excess, speaks the language of coercion and seeks to play the bully; "then fight against the one that oppresses" — against the one that speaks coercion, fight; "until it returns to the command of God" — until it is forced to return to God's command, until it is compelled to accept God's command. This is the command of God in the sphere of preserving unity within the Islamic society.

And as for the sphere of external relations: in the sphere of external relations, the Islamic world must strive — the Islamic Ummah must strive — to so order its relations with the non-Muslim world and with those outside this Ummah that it does not fall in the slightest under their command, does not come in the slightest under the influence of their thinking. That its independent politics, even slightly, do not lose their independence under the influence of their politics. Sharing a front and joining together with them is altogether forbidden — that is, when this joining together causes the Muslim Ummah to come under their influence.

There is a famous account here, whose details I will not now relate. It is a narration mentioned in most of the reliable books of the Shiʿa. It pertains to the time of Imam al-Bāqir(((Imam al-Bāqir))) — God's blessings be upon him — when the coinage of the Islamic world was being brought from the land of Rome (Byzantium)(((Byzantium))), and the Byzantine ruler made a threat, and they were left helpless and at a loss. Then the Imam, peace be upon him, guided the apparatus of the caliphate(((caliphate))). It is very strange! This is the only point — one or two exceptional cases — in which I see that the Imams of guidance, peace be upon them, showed a slight favorable face to the apparatus of the caliphate. One of them is here. The Imam guided the apparatus of the caliphate and instructed them that you should mint the coin in this way. They did not know how to mint coinage; they did not know the measure of the silver, where to obtain it and how to provide it — the measure of a dirham and a half-dirham and those things upon which the coin had to be struck. The Imam guided them.

Therefore, from the standpoint of external relations, the slightest receptivity to influence from anti-Islamic or non-Islamic factions — and especially the anti-Islamic — is forbidden. The Islamic society and the Islamic Ummah have no right to establish their bond with the world outside Islam except in the form of being the upper hand, being superior. That is, if a relationship is to be established between the Islamic Ummah and a non-Muslim Ummah, an exploitative relationship — suppose, for example, of the kind of the Tobacco affair(((Tobacco Protest))) and the Régie company,(((Régie company)))footnote:s24-3[On Nāṣir al-Dīn Shāh's third journey to Europe, a contract was concluded between the government of Iran and Gerald Talbot, founder of the Régie company, by which the concession for the trade in tobacco, pipe-tobacco, and cigarettes was given over to the monopoly of this company for a period of fifty years in exchange for the payment of a certain sum annually. Under the pretext of commercial activity, this company brought many foreign nationals into Iran, a considerable number of whom were Christian missionaries. This concession was annulled by the issuance of the prohibition decree (fatwa) of Mīrzā[-yi] Shīrāzī.] which you gentlemen know and have heard about — the Islamic world has no right to enter into this relationship. Or suppose, like those Mughal sultans(((Mughal Empire))) and rulers of India(((India))) who permitted foreign governments to come and establish companies there — they performed an act contrary to the Wilayat of the Islamic world. It was not permissible, this act of letting a company come; because they knew, or if they did not know, they should have known — they must know — that the company — for example, the East India Company(((East India Company)))footnote:s24-4[The East India Company was an English joint-stock company which, by receiving a charter from the Queen of England and exclusive concessions from the ruler of India, was able to attain enormous profit. Gradually, under the pretext of defending its property and so forth, this company stationed military forces in India and in practice made this great country part of the colonies of Britain.] — when it entered that region, when it entered that land, what calamity it would bring upon the lives of those people, and how it would spread the cancer of colonialism(((colonialism))) into the very veins and sinews of that vast subcontinent. They should have understood these things; they did not understand. And the Islamic world and the Islamic Ummah never permit relations of this kind.@@CX(Wilayah | the Ummah may never permit exploitative or colonial relations with the non-Muslim world)@@

Take note. When we say that relations are cut off with non-Muslim governments and with non-Muslim nations, this does not mean that the Islamic world and the Islamic Ummah will live in political isolation. No; the issue is not the issue of political isolation, such that you might imagine that the Islamic world has no commercial relation with anyone, no political relation, no diplomatic relations, neither sends an ambassador nor receives an ambassador — no, it is not like that; it has ordinary relations. It has no Wilayat with them, no attachment with them.@@CX(Wilayah | severing external bonds means no dependence, not political isolation)@@ It has no essential and substantial bondfootnote:s24-5[A matter relating to the foundation, the root, and the essence of each thing and each affair.] with them. It will not be such that, if they wish, they can place the Islamic world under their own influence.

So Wilayat has two facets — Qur'anic Wilayat. One facet is that within the Islamic society all elements must step toward one goal, toward one direction, by one path, with one footstep. Outside the Islamic society, the Islamic Ummah must sever its bonds with all the blocs and the anti-Islamic factions.

Here there exists a precise point which shows that Wilayat in this sense, which has come in the Qur'an, is in the very same sense of the Wilayat that we, the Shiʿa(((Shiʿa))), hold. Pay attention so that I may express this point, so that you all may understand how Wilayat in Shiʿism connects with Qur'anic Wilayat. It is possible that four persons may be present who, out of devotional acceptance, or [as] the past generation may have been present and, out of devotional acceptance, heard certain things about Wilayat and accepted them — but this young person who wishes to understand and is connected with the Qur'an must pay attention to where Shiʿi Wilayat comes from.

This [fact] that we have considered the connection with the Imam to be so important, this [fact] that we have considered the command of the Imam to be in force in all the affairs of the life of the society — what is this for? From where does it come? Here the Qur'an speaks to us — that point is the very thing I am saying — if a society and an Ummah wishes to possess Qur'anic Wilayat in this sense — that is, wishes for all its internal forces to set out in one direction, toward one goal, along one line, and wishes all its internal forces to be mobilized against the anti-Islamic powers abroad — it needs a point of concentrated power at the heart of the Islamic society. It needs a point to which all the internal forces are joined, from which all draw inspiration, and to whom all listen and obey, and who knows all aspects of the interests and harms, so that — like a strong, powerful-handed, keen-eyed watchman — he may assign each person on the battlefront to his own particular task. There must exist a leadership, a command, a concentrated power in the Islamic society, which knows what can be expected of you, what can be expected of me, what can be expected of other human beings, so that to each it may assign the task that is required of him, saying: act.@@CX(Leadership | the Ummah needs a single concentrated point of power that assigns each person his task)@@

For example, by way of analogy — if I want to make an analogy: have you seen these carpet-weaving workshops? A number of people are seated weaving a carpet; each is working — every child or grown person seated there is weaving thread so that by dusk he may receive two or three tomans. Each is expending some enthusiasm and labor here. If these works are not coordinated, if there is no higher thought and eye and power that continually reads out that pattern of the carpet according to those particular instructions, so that they may know which thread to use, and how to strike it, and how to cut it — if such a concentrated power does not exist, how will this carpet come out?! You will see that its right side tells of the East, and its left side tells of the West! One part speaks of a Kurdish carpet, another part speaks of a Turkmen carpet! The patterns disordered, it comes out something altogether absurd.

The orderliness of the carpet — this [feature] that you see, that a flower has appeared on this side, [and] like that flower, without any addition or subtraction, on this side too one has grown, has appeared, and both have reached that medallion (toranj) in the center, and just like these two, on that side of the carpet also there are two more, and all things are orderly and everything is in its place — what is this for? It is because, first, the instruction is determined, and one person too is seated, calling out in a loud voice — I don't know — "Bring two of those down from above, strike that thread from that side, cut that one from that side." Which I have never heard and do not know how to do; the carpet-sellers know it. The carpet-sellers too do not know it; the carpet-weavers know it. Did you notice?

In a society, if all the forces are to be put to work, and all to work in one direction, and none of the forces to go to waste, and all the forces of the society to be brought to bear, in the form of one concentrated power, upon the collective interests of humanity, and the society to be able to be like a single fist in the face of the opposing factions and ranks and powers — if it wishes to have these, it needs a concentrated power. It needs a heart, this society and this general body of the Islamic Ummah needs a heart. Of course, it also has conditions. He must be very aware, must know very much, must be very resolute, his eye must possess a different kind of vision, he must fear nothing on the path of God, he must, when necessary, sacrifice even himself. What do we name such a being? The Imam.@@CX(Leadership | the heart of the Ummah must be aware, resolute, fearless, and ready to sacrifice himself)@@

The Imam means that ruler and leader who is appointed in the society on the part of the Lord. This that I say, "on the part of God," what does it mean? It means either that God appoints [him] by name and designation — as He appointed the Commander of the Faithful(((ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib))), Imam Ḥasan(((Imam Ḥasan))), Imam Ḥusayn(((Imam Ḥusayn))), and the rest of the Imams; the Prophet himself too is an Imam; the Prophet himself too is an Imam:

God the Exalted says to Abraham(((Abraham))): I have made you an Imam. The Imam means that leader and ruler and guide in a society.

At one time God appoints this Imam by name and designation, saying: after the Prophet it must be ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him, for example. At another time God the Exalted does not appoint the Imam by name; He appoints [him] by a mark (sign).@@CX(Imamate | God appoints the Imam either by name and designation or by a mark)@@ Like what? Like the statement of the Imam, peace be upon him:footnote:s24-6[Imam Ḥasan al-ʿAskarī, hadith no. 46. "As for whoever among the jurists is one who guards his soul, preserves his religion, opposes his [base] desire, and obeys the command of his Master, then it is for the common people to follow him (do taqlīd of him)."] «fa-ammā man kāna mina al-fuqahāʾi ṣāʾinan li-nafsihi ḥāfiẓan li-dīnihi mukhālifan ʿalā hawāhu muṭīʿan li-amri mawlāhu fa-li-l-ʿawāmmi an yuqallidūhu» — the Imam has appointed [him], you see; this too is a designated Imam (imām manṣūṣ),footnote:s24-7[(n-ṣ-ṣ) designated by way of [Qur'anic] verses and [prophetic] narrations.] he is himself an Imam, he is an Imam. That jurist (faqīh)(((faqīh))) who is the successor [of the Imam] — but he is an Imam who has not been appointed by name; he has been appointed by a mark. Whoever this mark applies to, he becomes the Imam.@@CX(Imamate | the qualified jurist is an Imam appointed by a mark, not by name)@@ I wished to explain for you the word "Imam." The Imam means leader, means ruler, means the one who holds the reins, means the one whom — wherever he goes — human beings follow; who must be on the part of God, must be just, must be fair, must be religious, must be resolute, and the like of these statements which exist concerning the Imamate, which we are not now in a position [to discuss].

So the principle of Qur'anic Wilayat necessitated — what thing? The existence of the Imam. The discussion is not yet finished; pay attention. If this great body, whose name is the Islamic Ummah, wishes to remain alive, to remain successful, to be ever enduring, what must it do? Its connection with this center, with this moving and impassioned heart, must always — all of it, ever — be firm, powerful, established. So what does Wilayat mean? Let all pay close attention: the other dimension of Wilayat — what does it mean? It means the firm and powerful connection of each of the individuals of the Islamic Ummah, in every state, with that heart of the Ummah. Connection of what? Intellectual connection and practical connection. That is, taking one's pattern truly from him, truly following him in thoughts and visions, and truly following him in deeds and conduct and activities and movements.@@CX(Wilayah | the third dimension is each individual's firm intellectual and practical connection with the leader)@@

So what does the Wilayat of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib mean? It means that in your thoughts you be a follower of ʿAlī; in your deeds you be a follower of ʿAlī; that a powerful, firm, unbreakable bond has tied you to ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, peace be upon him, so that you do not become separated from ʿAlī. This is the meaning of Wilayat. Have you understood well what Wilayat is? It is here that we understand the meaning of this tradition: «wilāyatu ʿalī ibn abī ṭālibin ḥiṣnī»footnote:s24-8[Hadith no. 43. "The Wilayat of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib is my fortress; whoever enters my fortress is safe from my punishment."] — the Wilayat of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib is my fortress and stronghold — [said] on the part of God — «fa-man dakhala ḥiṣnī amina min ʿadhābī» — whoever enters this stronghold will remain safe and secure from the punishment of God. It is a very interesting statement. What does it mean? It means that the Muslims, the followers of the Qur'an — if both from the standpoint of thought and reflection and from the standpoint of deed and effort and activity they are connected to ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib — they are safe and protected from God's punishment. Is it other than this? Is it anything but this? If ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib is recognized today, [and] after he is recognized, you and I act like him, then we attain Wilayat; Wilayat means this.

A servant [of God] who I deem incapable of understanding the Qur'an — of course I myself do [understand it]; God forbid that I should deem the Qur'an incapable of being understood; [I mean] the one who does not — that servant of God who deems the Qur'an incapable of being understood, how can he say "I have the Wilayat of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib and I am connected with ʿAlī from the standpoint of thought," while ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib says in a sermon of the Nahj al-Balāgha(((Nahj al-Balāgha))): «wa-aʿlamū anna hādhā al-qurʾāna huwa al-nāṣiḥu alladhī lā yaghushshu wa-l-hādī alladhī lā yuḍillu wa-l-muḥaddithu alladhī lā yakdhibu wa-mā jālasa hādhā al-qurʾāna aḥadun illā qāma ʿanhu bi-ziyādatin aw nuqṣānin: ziyādatin fī hudan aw nuqṣānin min ʿaman»footnote:s24-9[Hadith no. 24. "And know that this Qur'an is the counselor who does not deceive, and the guide who does not lead astray, and the teller who does not lie. No one sits with this Qur'an but that he rises from it with an increase or a decrease: an increase in guidance, or a decrease in [his] blindness."] — the Commander of the Faithful is in this manner referring people to the Qur'an, in this manner driving people toward the Qur'an; that servant of God who says "the Qur'an cannot be understood," does he have the Wilayat of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib? Never. ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib is willing, for the sake of God, to give up his entire being; the conduct of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib is this. This fellow is not willing to give up a single mithqāl of his money, of his life, of his social standing, of his comfort, of his lordliness, for the sake of God — does he have the Wilayat of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib? The Wilayat of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib is possessed by the one who is joined to ʿAlī with an unbreakable bond — both from the standpoint of thought and reflection, and from the standpoint of deed.@@CX(the Qur'an | one cannot claim ʿAlī's Wilayat while deeming the Qur'an impossible to understand)@@

If you reflect well, brothers, this meaning which I have given of Wilayat is the most precise and most subtle meaning that can be expounded, that can be expressed, concerning Wilayat; and this too emerges from the Qur'an. Now listen so that I may read the verses of the Qur'an for you, for in these verses of the Qur'an from Sūrat al-Mā'idah, eloquent allusions have been made to all these things: to the positive aspect of Wilayat — that is, the internal bond; to the negative aspect of Wilayat — that is, the severing of external bonds; and to that other dimension of Wilayat — that is, the connection and link with the walī (the walī meaning that pole, meaning that heart, meaning that ruler and Imam). Now pay attention so that the matter may be grasped.@@CX(Wilayah | Sūrat al-Mā'idah alludes to all three aspects of Wilayat — positive, negative, and the bond with the walī)@@

"O you who have believed" — O you who have believed; "do not take the Jews and the Christians as allies (awliyāʾ)" — do not take the Jews and the Christians, the Jewish and Christian peoples, as your awliyāʾ. Awliyāʾ is the plural of walī; walī is from wilayat; wilayat means attachment, walī means one who is attached and bonded. Do not take the Jews and the Christians as those bonded and attached to yourselves; do not choose them. "Some of them are allies of one another (baʿḍuhum awliyāʾu baʿḍ)" — they are awliyāʾ of one another, sharers of a front and attached to one another. Do not look at the fact that their blocs are separate from each other; in meaning, for the sake of enmity against your authenticities, they are all one front. "Baʿḍuhum awliyāʾu baʿḍ" — some are of the same front as others. "And whoever is an ally to them among you (wa-man yatawallahum minkum)" — whoever takes them as his walī — tawallī means accepting Wilayat, of the form tafaʿʿul — whoever sets foot in the valley of their Wilayat and bonds himself with them, connects himself with them, establishes a relationship; "then indeed, he is one of them (fa-innahu minhum)" — without doubt he himself is one of them. "Indeed, God does not guide the wrongdoing people (inna-llāha lā yahdī al-qawma al-ẓālimīn)" — and God will not guide the wrongdoing people.

"So you see those in whose hearts is disease (fa-tarā alladhīna fī qulūbihim maraḍ)" — those in whose hearts there is disease, which we have translated "the sick of heart"; "fa-tarā alladhīna fī qulūbihim maraḍ" — those who in their heart have disease and sickness, do you see them?(((hypocrisy))) "Hastening into [allegiance with] them (yusāriʿūna fīhim)" — they hasten amid the front of the enemies of religion; they are not content merely to go toward them, but they hasten; they are not content merely to go up beside them — they go all the way into the depths of their front. If you ask, "Sir, why do you make so [much] friendship with the enemy of religion? With someone whom you know to be against religion, why do you not only not oppose him at all, but even expend friendship?" If you ask this of him, he answers you thus: "Saying, 'We fear lest a turn of fortune strike us (yaqūlūna nakhshā an tuṣībanā dāʾirah)'" — they say: we fear that some harm may reach us; if I do not befriend [them], I fear they will inflict some harm on me, or [someone] will inflict it. How familiar these words are to one's ear! "Nakhshā an tuṣībanā dāʾirah" — we fear that some trouble will be made for us, we fear that some occasion of distress will be made for us.
