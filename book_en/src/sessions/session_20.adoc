The prophets, with that very lofty and progressive aim that we explained on the previous days — an aim that, even by the cultural standards of the age of light and the age of human awareness, that is, the twentieth century, is the most progressive of all aims — namely the aim of bringing humankind into one class, of leveling and equalizing human beings, and of casting out ignorance and poverty and oppression and exploitation and class division — with this most precious and progressive and lofty aim, the prophets(((Prophethood, nubuwwah))) were commissioned and set foot in their societies.@@CX(Prophethood | aim is to level human beings and abolish class division)@@

A continuous chain of activity and striving and struggle is the harvest of the lives of God's prophets. From the very beginning of the mission, never again to sleep in comfort, never again to slumber on the bed of ease,footnote:s20-3[Literally, "never to let the eye grow warm with sleep" — that is, never to close the eyes in restful sleep.] never again to taste pleasure and rest, but to struggle unceasingly — this is the summary of the story of the divine prophets. At the end of their lives, too — as we have learned from the religious sources — some had their heads severed from their bodies by the tyrants and despots of the age and carried off as a trophy; some were placed within a tree and sawn from head to foot, cut in two; some passed from this world in exile and in distress. None of them amassed any wealth, however little; none of them, at the end of their lives, left behind a palace, an apparatus, and vast riches, as the rulers of this world and the worldly leaders do. This is the summary of the lives of the prophets and the envoys and the deputed ones and the messengers of God; these things we have come to know.

A question now arises. As it stands in our minds, and as we read in the history of the prophetic missions, the prophets all passed their lives in struggle and striving, and many of them drank the draught of martyrdom. So was their work, then, a fruitless and useless work? Did the prophets meet defeat in history? Is it as the simple-minded of the world still imagine it to be — and as the anti-prophetic powers, too, are not displeased to have lodged in the minds of the common people — that the prophets gained no fruitful result from their striving, and that across the whole of history, oppression and tyranny and inhumanity and rebellion and unbelief have held sway? Is it so?

We believe that it is not so. We believe that these dear deputies of God — whose chain began with Adam(((Adam))) and Noah(((Noah))) and Abraham(((Abraham))), and in whose chain the choicest of creation came one after another — these men not only were not defeated, not only did not fail, not only did not see what they said and what they wanted in the world, in history, in society meet with defeat; rather, among all of humanity, and among all those who have ever run after some aim and goal and striven for it, no one has had the chance of success that the prophets had. This is our belief. We believe that the outcome of prophethood and the end of the prophets' work, throughout all history, in the time that is past, has ended according to what was desired — and so it will be in the future too. And we shall prove this.@@CX(Prophethood | the outcome of prophethood has always ended as desired)@@

There are two matters and two topics here. (I ask the brothers who have heard this series of discussions from the previous days to pay careful attention, so that the important part of this chapter may be connected to the earlier discussions; and the brothers who, it seems, were not here on the other days and have come today — let them attend well to this very topic again, for in itself it is a topic that must be grasped and known. But you must pay attention.)

We have two matters here. The first is this: this chain that is known by the name of prophethood and messengership — that is, the caravan of the prophets from Adam to the Seal [of the Prophets] — what did they do? What did the totality of them accomplish? Did they push things forward, or did they go bankrupt? This is one matter. The other matter is this: each of the great divine prophets, in his own time — did he succeed, or did he meet defeat? These are two questions, and I would like to finish both of them today, God willing. By your own measure, if you reflect, you will find that you would like to understand this matter. This is something the knowing of which is beneficial to us — by the very criterion I have always placed in your hands.

It is beneficial in the sense that it not only increases our information — for there are many words that increase a person's information but are of no benefit to him. There is no harm at all in all of you, this whole gathering, now being aware of how the tiny particles of the rocks in the volcanic mountains of the moon are composed. To know it — is that bad? But is it beneficial? No. Much of the information that we run after, or that they make us run after, is of this sort. After we have sat and listened and spoken and studied and researched and put pen to paper and brought our life to its end, then when we look, we see a heap of empty information, useless information, that did not bring us a single step closer to Paradise and to the good pleasure of God, nor a single step closer to the sound, authentic Islamic society in this world; it did nothing at all. Yes, it had only this benefit: that we could pride ourselves, could believe that we are very learned, very knowledgeable, and could close upon ourselves the window of receptivity and learn nothing more from anyone; this was its only benefit.

This matter that I am presenting is beneficial in the true sense; it brings not only awareness but awareness and commitment. It is of that kind of faith that I was explaining and speaking of to you here on the third and fourth and fifth of the month. The first question is this: this chain whose members are called the prophets — from first to last, what flower did they pin upon the head of humanity? The answer is this: the prophets came and took a being who could not even, at the level of an animal, distinguish the road from the pit — a being in whom even instinct was not so capable and path-opening — and they brought this being up to the level of a being from whom even the angels of heaven must come and learn something. They brought humankind from the lowest depthfootnote:s20-4[ḥaḍīḍ (root ḥ-ḍ-ḍ): a lowering, a lowliness, the nadir.] of savagery and ignorance up to the level of a civilized human being, such that if he wishes to act according to their teachings, the loftiest, the most beautiful, the most eloquent manifestations of creation will appear in his life.@@CX(Prophethood | raised humankind from savagery to civilization and perfection)@@

The prophets treated human beings like a schoolchild — I am trying to bring the matter down to a very low level — who knows nothing, not even the alphabet. For one year they labored over him, and he came to the second grade; but the teacher of the first grade, in the very midst of this,footnote:s20-5[athnāʾ (root th-n-y): in the midst, in the interval, during.] when the child had reached the second grade, had laid down his own life over this work. They labored for another period, and he came to the third grade; but the teacher who had brought him from the second to the third grade lost his own life on this path. They labored for another period, and he came to the fourth grade; and again that compassionate teacher, that kind father, that aware guide — over the course of this time during which he was raising the pupil to a higher grade, to a higher rank — lost his own life, departed from this world, and passed his life in hardship. Grade by grade, step by step, stage by stage, they kept bringing this little child up, and up, and up. Now, when you look, you see that the level of his culture, his knowledge, his insight, his sensitivity is far higher than in that time which was two periods before the present, and he is at the peak of progress and elevation and comprehension and understanding and thought.

You look, and the teachers are not there. Where are the teachers? One, in the midst of the first grade, while he was bringing this slow-witted pupil up — let us say — gave up his life. Another, while these very pupils were learning something from him, at the end of the year, over some disagreement, over some matter, gathered together, rose up against him, and killed him. The teacher of the third grade in another way, the teacher of the fourth grade in another way. The teachers are not there; each teacher carried out his mission and, to outward appearance, died in failure and perished. But did he really fail? Think well; see whether that teacher really failed. What, after all, was the teacher's aim? Did not this compassionate teacher want exactly this? He wanted this pupil to rise from the black dust to the summit of the peak of culture and knowledge and understanding — and has this not now come to pass?

So the teachers did not die as failures. It is true that they died, it is true that they did not attain the affluent, comfortable life of this world, it is true that they did not see with their own eyes this sapling come to fruit — but did they fail? No, they did not fail. Their aim was precisely this: that this human being should traverse the first and second grade; that this ignorant, unaware student should go up this road with difficulty, with hardship, with toil, until he reached that summit of the peak — and now he has reached it.

The prophets were just like this: Adam, Noah, Lot, Hud, Salih, Shuʿayb, Abraham, Ishmael, Moses(((Moses))), Jesus, and thousands of other prophets throughout history — their work was this: to bring this human being up, to teach him knowledge and understanding, to prepare him for ordering the life to come, to give him the necessary substance. And this work was accomplished — although, along the way, a number of these prophets were killed in a horrible manner, in a pitiable form, and departed from this world without seeing this end. And still humanity is moving forward.

Today the world, more than it was a thousand years ago, is prepared to hear the true word of Islam. Today humanity is more ready to accept the divine government than it was a thousand years ago, than it was twelve or thirteen hundred years ago — and a thousand years from now it will be more ready than today. The day that our Imam of the Age(((Imam of the Age))) — may God hasten his noble relief — went into occultation from sight, and as an Imam who must spread out the carpet [of justice] among the people does not spread it out, on that day humankind was not ready to accept a revolutionary reforming Imam with sword in hand.@@CX(Wilayah | the Imam went into occultation because humanity was not yet ready)@@ And if that noble one had wanted to rise up and make a revolution and overturn society, he certainly could not, given the unfavorable conditions of that time, have built a society such as he desired.

The experience of the noble Imams of the Prophet's household had reached the point that this society had become so corrupted, the oppressive and tyrannical hands of the despotic powers, of the Umayyads(((Umayyads))) and the Abbasids(((Abbasids))), had so worked this field for the worse and poisoned it and corrupted it, that no sound plant could any longer be grown in it; and so the Imam, peace be upon him, was hidden from sight. The day he comes — how will it be? The day he will appear — and we do not know whether it is ten years away or ten thousand years away, nothing is known at all — on that day, when the Imam appears, humanity will have reached readiness; it will be ready to hear and to accept the true word of Islam. It will be ready to carry the elevated and lofty Islamic society upon its own shoulders. On that day humanity will be ready for the Qur'an to be put into practice. But in the time of the Imam of the Age it was not ready — and who has done this work? The teachings of the prophets have done it, and the Imams are the followers of the prophets.@@CX(Wilayah | the Imams are followers of the prophets and continue their work)@@

In the discussion of the Imamate, when I expound the philosophy of the Imamate, I will explain what the philosophy of the Imam's existence is; I will make this clear. So the prophets were not unsuccessful in history, and we see that every day humanity has moved forward and added to its own age, it has come one step closer to the peak of progress and perfection and elevation. What more do we want than this? What do the prophets want? The Lord of the worlds wants to bring this unripe, raw substance, by a natural movement, toward its natural and innate destination — that is, perfection and elevation. This the Lord of the worlds has willed, and humanity will certainly reach that point of ultimate perfection. And this is the determinism of history; the determinism of history means just this, and in this form he [who used the phrase] spoke correctly.

These are precise discussions; I ask you to pay attention, to the very words and expressions. The course of humankind is toward elevation and perfection, and toward the promised paradise of this world; that is, humanity will see a day for itself in which, on that day, there will be no oppression, no evil, no ugliness — everything will be according to the desire of humanity. The creation of the human being is like this, and the creation of the world; that this being, in its own course, will at last arrive at such a destination — it must arrive. In that era when humanity will have ready all that is necessary for its perfection, then it will enter the channel of progress and perfection and elevation, and with the utmost speed and pace it will move toward God — that is, toward absolute perfection. And the more we have come forward from the beginning of human history up to this time of ours, the closer we have drawn toward that destination; this is the determinism of history. The creation of the human being is like this, and the creation of the world; God has so created that human beings move toward progress and elevation, willy-nilly. As for the "willy-nilly," you must of course derive its meaning in the course of these discussions — what "willy-nilly" means — for later, when we tie it to the subsequent discussion, it will become clear what it means. Of course the will of human beings is intensely involved, and it is the wanting of human beings that brings human beings to progress, and this wanting will be there.

This is one of the principles among the Islamic principles; in the Islamic worldview, this is among the certainties: that the end of humankind is in accordance with goodness.@@CX(Attaining perfection | the end of humankind accords with goodness)@@ Why? Because God Most High created the earth and the heavens in accordance with truth; and He created the human being too with a truth-seeking innate nature. Since the human being possesses will, he must move along the path that accords with his innate nature, in order to reach that destination. And who can present this path to him and tell him: if you do such-and-such, you have acted in accordance with your innate nature? The prophets — the prophets come for this. They come to show humankind the path of innate nature; that is, to hasten and ease his course toward a good end.@@CX(fiṭrah | prophets come to show humankind the path of its innate nature)@@ Therefore human beings are moving forward; humanity is drawing day by day closer to felicity and to the destination of goodness, and this is because of the movement that the prophets gave it. The prophets set it in motion; if some delay has occurred in the middle of history, and in each segment and stage of the stages of history, it is because of a relative distancing from the teachings of the prophets — but in the end they had this course. This is one matter in general.

In the first question, we draw this conclusion: we say that the great divine prophets, although each one of them individually met with deprivations and failures, yet in the aggregate, the course of humanity has been toward progress and elevation, and its agent was the prophets. It was the prophets who pushed the human being toward that intended destination and toward the kneaded-in human end, set him in motion, helped him in traversing this road; this is the first question.

But now the second question — the one that is of greater interest to many — this is the second question that I shall make clear for you. The second question is this: when a prophet brings about a movement in the world, raises up a revolution and an uprising, can it be said that this revolution has a good outcome, or not? Can one hope that the end of this work is good? Or can one not hope? What is the general rule here?

Some say: however much we look, we see that wherever a true word issued from a tongue, wherever the melody of truth reached the ear from some quarter, in the end it did not reach result and fruit, and it was choked off. From this they have constructed an "experience"; they say: the experience of human history has always shown us that the prophets failed. Now, even granting that you say overall they succeeded — fine — but in the end the revolution they brought about, they were unable to bring it to fruit. Not in a single place were they able to bring truth to power and destroy falsehood. So then, what should we do? Should we too, after this, not raise a hand, not draw it from our sleeve, not follow the path of the prophets? Since the great divine prophets themselves, in their own time, were unable to accomplish anything, and in the end falsehood has always — even if seasonally and by stages — gained the upper hand over truth, then let the truth-seekers and the truth-speakers of the world sheathe their swords, go and lie down to sleep, and know that it is of no use — unless some hand should come out of some sleeve, a hand of power, an unseen hand should come forth and do something.

This is the logic of many people, and this is the very logic that I mentioned: the tyrants(((Tāghūt))) of history take great pleasure in this logic.@@CX(Tāghūt | tyrants want people to believe striving in truth is always futile)@@ This is the very logic that the bullies of the age have always loved the people to hold as their belief — that is, that the people should believe that, sir, no striving, no activity, no struggle in the path of truth will ever reach a result. This the tyrants of the world have always wanted.

The political bluffs of the heads of the states of the world, which you read so much of in the newspapers that carry world news, are for precisely this purpose. That certain man, who has only a few hours or a few days left before the throne of his rule collapses and he comes down from the peak of the presidency to become an ordinary man — that very man, even a few days before, is bluffing: "We will crush our enemies, we will wipe out our opponents, we will continue to rule" — just as, quite recently in the world and on a very vast global scale, you yourselves saw with your own eyes, read, were informed: a power of such magnitude suddenly comes down, turns into an ordinary man, but a little before this action and reaction, this transfer and transformation, he says, "They cannot, they cannot, they cannot touch us, our position will not be lost" — and then we see that it was lost. These bluffs are for precisely this purpose. They always wish to make the people understand in this way: that nothing can come from your hands, because our power is a power that you cannot even imagine. All means and resources are at our disposal, and you have none.

Do you imagine that these things have arisen in the world only today? No; they have existed at all times in history. This is a very interesting and amusing affair in history that, whenever I recall it, I am amazed at the cunning of al-Mansur the Abbasid. Al-Mansur the Abbasid(((al-Mansur the Abbasid))) was, as you know, the greatest and most powerful caliph of the Abbasids, and the second Abbasid caliph. As it happened, in al-Mansur's time, opposition had begun from the margins of the religious community of that day. Among those who had placed al-Mansur under severe pressure were Muhammad ibn ʿAbdullah and Ibrahim ibn ʿAbdullah, the two sons of ʿAbdullah al-Mahd(((ʿAbdullah al-Mahd))).footnote:s20-6[ʿAbdullah ibn al-Hasan al-Muthanna, known as ʿAbdullah al-Maḥḍ ("the pure"); so called because his father, al-Hasan al-Muthanna, was a son of Imam al-Hasan, and his mother, Fatima, was a daughter of Imam al-Husayn — for which reason he was called a pure (khāliṣ) ʿAlawid. Two of his sons, named Muhammad — known as al-Nafs al-Zakiyya ("the Pure Soul") — and Ibrahim, rose in opposition against al-Mansur al-Dawaniqi and went into hiding in a secret place. To learn their whereabouts, al-Mansur treated ʿAbdullah al-Maḥḍ very harshly and imprisoned him several times in Medina and Kufa. After the uprising of his sons, al-Mansur severed both their heads from their bodies and sent them to him. ʿAbdullah was killed at the age of 75, by order of al-Mansur, in the year 145 AH.] These two men struggled fiercely against al-Mansur. Al-Mansur had been rendered helpless. Although everything was at al-Mansur's disposal and these two self-sacrificing brothers had nothing at their disposal but faith and a handful of believers, nevertheless, in the face of their relentless striving and activity and struggle, al-Mansur had lost his footing. Many rumors, concerning the power and might of al-Mansur's apparatus, were being spread among the people, with the sole aim of preventing the true believers and the genuine Shiʿa from joining these two brothers. Among them was this: al-Mansur had spread the rumor that he had a mirror, and that when he looked into this mirror he could tell where Ibrahim ibn ʿAbdullah was — and woe to that man into whose house al-Mansur, looking into the mirror, should see that Ibrahim was, or with whom he was fleeing. The story of the mirror — the very same one that, a thousand, two thousand years before al-Mansur, those tyrant kings of history, Jamshid and this one and that one, fashioned in this form as the world-revealing cup. No doubt this too was for the same purpose: that the enemies should know and imagine that they could never carry out any deed outside the sight of these powers. They had even resorted to such things.

Therefore the tyrants of the world ask of the God of the two worlds — a God in whom they do not believe — that the people should imagine that the prophetic missions and the great divine prophets and the envoys of truth and reality have always been doomed to defeat. They ask of God that the people should imagine and suppose that no truth-worshipping melody will ever receive a favorable and positive response from the nature of this world. They ask of God that the people should despair of any movement ever growing and reaching fruit against falsehood without being defeated. But is the reality of the matter really like this? Because a number of minds under the sway [of these powers] and uninformed have imagined it so, because the people have supposed that the prophets and the followers of the prophets and the Imams have always been defeated and that every movement in the world will be defeated — are we too obliged to accept it? Should we set aside the Qur'an? Should we not hear correctly the word of the Qur'an, which recounts to us the opposite of this logic, and instead hear the word of false fancies? No; we will not do this. We turn to the Qur'an(((the Qur'an))); we take a lesson from the Qur'an as to what the outcome of the prophetic missions is, and what effect the revolutionary movement of the prophets and their followers can have.@@CX(the Qur'an | the Qur'an, not false fancy, teaches the true outcome of prophethood)@@ Let us learn this from the Qur'an, then we return to the textfootnote:s20-7[naṣṣ (root n-ṣ-ṣ): text.] of history, and we see that history too bears witness to this matter.

The Qur'an strikes a parable in these very verses that I recited, and which I now recite again and translate; it tells us: No! In the world there is a truth and there is a falsehood; the truth remains, the falsehood is annihilated. From the mountains, in the rivers, water flows and streams; froth appears on the surface of the water; the froth dies, the waters remain. The verses of Sūrat al-Ṣaffāt too we have rendered in these writings; they too say this very same thing. _"And Our word has already gone forth to Our servants, the messengers"_footnote:s20-19[Sūrat al-Ṣaffāt (the Blessed Sūra of al-Ṣaffāt), verses 171 to 173.] — already Our command for Our deputed servants, that is, the prophets, has passed and been concluded; beforehand We took the requisite and necessary decision concerning them. What is that decision? _"that they will surely be the ones helped"_ — they are the ones helped; they are helped by Us alone. _"And that Our hosts will surely be the victors"_ — it is Our soldiers who become victorious.
