This is an issue that must absolutely be raised within the discussions of Prophethood(((Prophethood, nubuwwah))), and if we fail to understand it and do not grasp it, then much of what has been discussed on the subject of Prophethood will have almost no practical effect for us. This last discussion is a guarantee for the previous discussions to be put into practice and to enter into our lives.@@CX(Prophethood | faith in it must enter practical life)@@

What is this final discussion? It is this: we who say _"Ashhadu anna Muḥammadan rasūl Allāh"_ — "I bear witness that Muhammad is the Messenger of God" — I announce, in my call to prayer, in my ritual prayer, in the act of pronouncing the two testimonies (the _shahādatayn_), everywhere, that I am of the Prophet's Ummah(((Ummah))), and I accept, I am convinced, I have faith in his prophethood — this testimony that I give, what commitment does it place upon my shoulders? Does this belief, this testimony, this profession, create any commitment for me at all, or not?@@CX(Faith | the shahāda must create commitment)@@

There is one kind of testimony: you say, "I bear witness and accept that — suppose — the narcissus flower is more fragrant than the Muhammadan rose." That is one matter. Some people do not even know this; some believe the opposite. You, sir, whether rightly or wrongly, have become convinced that this flower's scent is more pleasing to the nose and better than that flower. Well, now that you have given this testimony — _thumma mādhā_, as we seminary students say, "and then what?" Nothing. You see that if a person testifies and accepts that this flower is better than that flower, or, conversely, accepts that that flower is better than this flower, it has no effect on our life, it creates no commitment in our life.

Let me give another example. In the Christian world(((Christianity))), one person stands at the head of the religious hierarchy, called the Pope(((Pope))). As you know, this Pope is in reality merely a kind of statue that people must respect. He does not put forth any new theory in Christian beliefs, nor does he issue any new ruling among Christian laws that differs from previous rulings; his being or not being is just like the presence or absence of a beautiful statue in your reception room — if it is there, the room's décor is complete; if it is not there, then in the eyes of luxury-loving people the room is deficient. The existence of the Pope and his absence have no greater effect than this on the Christian world from the standpoint of Christian thought. Now, in this very year in which we are living, if an informed Christian were to be aware that the present Pope has passed away and so-and-so has taken his place, or were not to believe it, it would have no effect whatsoever on his life. If a Christian were to say, "I bear witness that the existing Pope of my time is Mr. So-and-so," this testimony brings him no commitment.@@CX(Faith | a testimony that imposes no duty is empty)@@ That is to say, the state of this Christian and the state of that broken-down old man sitting in a corner of some village — who has no news that this former Pope has died and that someone has come in his place — the state of this one is the same as the state of that uninformed man cast off in his corner. Just as the latter's life situation underwent no change after the death of the former Pope, so too in the life situation of this gentleman who knows the Pope died and another has taken his seat, no change of any kind comes about. "I bear witness that today the Pope is so-and-so" — this testimony creates no commitment.

Now: when I say in the _tashahhud_ of my prayer, _"Ashhadu anna Muḥammadan rasūl Allāh,"_ when it is proclaimed from the minaret of my city and province as a general slogan, as a portrayer of the overall countenance of this society, _"Ashhadu anna Muḥammadan rasūl Allāh"_ — this testimony to prophethood, this faith in prophethood and the proclaiming of this faith, does it or does it not place a commitment upon my shoulders as the one professing it, upon the shoulders of that society for which this profession is uttered as its slogan? This is the question.

The answer is this: yes, it does. What commitment does it place upon the shoulder of the follower of the Prophet and the acceptor of his call? In a single phrase, I summarize this commitment: the commitment, the responsibility, that a person who believes in the prophethood of the Prophet takes upon his shoulder consists of following the path of the Prophet and accepting the responsibility of carrying the Prophet's burden home.@@CX(Prophethood | commitment is to follow the Prophet's path and carry his burden)@@ These words are uttered very easily, but their responsibility is very heavy; and fundamentally, to be of the Prophet's Ummah and to be a witness to his prophethood is precisely this.

A group of people imagine that if they have said "We accept that so-and-so is a prophet" — that as soon as they have proclaimed with the tongue the acceptance of his prophethood that is in their heart — their work is done, and they have brought themselves across the border of Hell into the border of Heaven. Listen carefully; see whether this belief I am describing exists in your mind too, or not. I have no concern with who holds this belief and who does not. Some imagine that human beings were burning in the fire of God's wrath and fury, and then the matter of the prophethood of the Seal of the Prophets was raised; a number of these people who were in the region of torment and the precinct of God's wrath said _"Ashhadu anna Muḥammadan rasūl Allāh"_ — "I bear witness that Muhammad is the Prophet of God" — and by uttering this phrase they came out of the region of God's torment, they were pulled out, and they fell into the region of mercy. Now, if they prayed, they come a little closer, within the region of mercy, to the wellspring of mercy; if they fasted, they come a little closer; if they paid _khums_ and _zakāt_, they come a little closer; if they also enjoined good and forbade evil, they come a little bit closer; if they performed other deeds too, then they reach the wellspring of mercy; and even if they did not perform them, in the end they are still within the region of mercy. Did you grasp it? Some imagine it this way.

The result of this way of thinking is that if today a person were to write in his identity document "Muslim, follower of the Prophet of Islam" — back when they used to write it in identity documents, though now they do not — or, in a question and answer put to him, when they ask, "Sir, what is your creed and religion?" and he said "Islam"; merely because he has specified Islam and not specified Christianity, not specified materialism(((materialism))), not specified Judaism(((Judaism))), not specified some other religion — merely because, among these various things and items presented to him under the name of religion, he has put his finger on Islam, they say: "Very well; since you have specified Islam, go to Heaven.@@CX(Faith | mistaken belief that mere profession secures salvation)@@ Now, if you also prayed, all the better; if you also fasted, all the better; if you also did other deeds, all the better; and even if you did not do them, your place is in God's Heaven — except that before you reach the Resurrection, they will squeeze you hard." This is a saying and a thought that is current in people's minds.

We say this saying is not correct. Faith in the Prophet is necessary, but faith in the Prophet brings commitments along with it; if those commitments are fulfilled, then to whatever extent those commitments are fulfilled, to that same extent a person's faith is sound.@@CX(Faith | soundness of faith measured by fulfilled commitments)@@ If a person held a faith on his tongue, or even in his heart — a faith — but did not commit himself to any of the commitments that this faith specifies for a person, did not accept any of the responsibilities that acceptance of the Prophet's prophethood places upon a person's shoulder, then this person, although outwardly a believer, is not a true believer.@@CX(Faith | outward believer without commitment is not a true believer)@@ What will God do with him at the Resurrection? I do not know, nor do I have any concern to know; but by the measures of this world, as a matter of judgment, as a basis on which we can pass a verdict on the faith or lack of faith of a human being, we do not regard this man as a person of faith.

Of course, let me also add this: the utterance of this phrase and the outward declaration of belief — although it preserves a person's life and property, as the saying goes (though of course there is a bit of loose usage in this expression), that is, it brings a person inside the Islamic sphere — but the discussion is not about whether we should see whether a person's life and property are protected or not. We want to see whether he is a believer or not a believer. On the basis of the Quranic criteria, whose verses I will now explain for you, we say: that human being who is not bound to the commitments of faith is not a believer. A believer is one who is bound to the commitments and responsibilities that faith in prophethood brings for a person.@@CX(Faith | a believer is one bound to faith's responsibilities)@@

What is this commitment? This commitment is that I should see what the Prophet wanted to do in this world. The Prophet wanted to lift a tremendous burden, the Prophet wanted to uproot a tremendous boulder, the Prophet wanted to erect a tremendous edifice. And I too, in my own time, should look and see: has that burden which the Prophet wanted to lift been completely lifted? Has that stone which the Prophet wanted to uprootfootnote:s22-2[(q-l-ʿ) to uproot, to tear out from the root.] from the ground and completely tear out been completely torn out? Has that edifice which the Prophet wanted to demolish and to build been built? If I saw that the answer is negative — that is, that burden is still on the ground, that stone has not been torn out, that edifice has not been built — let me strive to do that work which he wanted to do. Let me strive to lift this burden myself. If my bones were weak, if my strength did not suffice, then to the extent that I can, let me strain at it, and let me also find ten other people so that we lift this burden together; let me find yet another group so that we build this edifice together. If I could not finish the building, can I not at least bring ten bricks and stack them there? Can I not dig out even a portion of its foundation and footing? Can I not at least prepare the preliminaries of the work? If I say I cannot, it is a lie.@@CX(Prophethood | each believer must continue the Prophet's unfinished work)@@

He must be bound to this commitment; otherwise, if he were not bound to this commitment, it is a lie for him to say, "I bear witness that he is the Prophet of God." This is a superficial testimony.@@CX(Faith | the hypocrite professes with the tongue but not the heart)@@ "I bear witness that he is a prophet, but I cannot bear witness that I believe in his prophethood" — like the hypocrites(((hypocrites, munāfiqūn))):

They would come and say, "O Messenger of God, we bear witness that you are the Prophet of God." God says: yes, We Ourselves know that you are Our prophet; this matter is clear to Us, in Our view certain — the statement is, in itself, true. But We bear witness that they, in this testimony of theirs, were liars; in their hearts they do not accept this matter, while with their tongues they are saying it.

The commitment of the messengership is the building of a world in the form that Islam has stated; this is the commitment of the messengership.@@CX(Prophethood | mission is to build a world to Islam's dimensions)@@ The Messenger, when he comes, comes in order to build a world to the dimensions that Islam proposes. The Prophet is commissioned for this — so that he may set right the form of life and the order of life of human beings in the manner that God states. If you, in your own time, saw that humanity is not living by the God-ordained form, saw that mankind is deprived of having a divine society, saw that various schools are pulling humanity this way and that and that Islam has remained confined to a corner of people's minds and a corner of their hearts and no more — then your duty and your commitment, on the basis of the testimony you give to the messengership of the Prophet, is to strive to bring the world into the form ordained by Islam. This is the responsibility and the commitment of prophethood.@@CX(Prophethood | believer's duty to strive for a divine society)@@

Just as has been written in these papers, which you have studied or are studying: Islam brings a new thought; on the basis of this new thought, a new front-formation comes into being, a new lining-up comes into being. And we have repeatedly arrived at this conclusion, through the study of the verses of the Quran(((Qur'an))), that religion, in essence, means the creation of a new front-formation and lining-up.@@CX(Prophethood | religion creates a new front-formation in society)@@

When human beings are living in an ignorant (_jāhilī_) society(((jāhiliyyah))), the Prophet, when he comes into this society, divides into two groups the human beings who are all obedient and tamed, the human beings who are all facing one direction and one way; one group he rescues from this misguidance, this straying, this bewilderment, he changes their path, and they become two groups.@@CX(Prophethood | prophets are an agent of division within a uniformly misguided society)@@ In the sense I have described, the prophets are an agent of division; in this sense, the prophets are an agent of duality. Remember the sense in which I said this, so that if you wish to recount it somewhere, you do not say "So-and-so says that the Prophet is an agent of division." In this sense the Prophet is an agent of division; in which sense? In this way: everyone is going, like the cars of a single train, toward the downward slope of ruin; the Prophet comes and takes hold of these cars from behind. Some of the cars detach themselves from the Prophet's hand and go toward the downward slope of ruin; a number welcome this holding-back of the Prophet's; division falls among the cars.

This caravan is going toward the bandits, is going toward the place of death, is going toward the earthquake-zone; the Prophet comes and says, "Do not go." A number listen to his word and do not go; a number do not listen and go. So they become two fronts — division. In this sense, the prophets, when they come, create division and duality among societies — except that it is a duality within a society that was uniformly misguided. They come and say, "Turn back toward God"; among those uniform masses division falls; some turn back, some do not turn back.

So a new front, a new lining-up, a new mutual positioning comes into being in society with the coming of the Prophet. The Prophet on one side, in one rank; the enemies and opponents and the obstinate adversaries of the Prophet on the other side, in another rank. Pay close attention to these two ranks that I am sketching. The Prophet was at first all alone; everyone was in that opposing rank. The Prophet has exerted effort, has striven, one by one, two by two, until at last he has been able to form a rank facing that misguided and hellbound rank; to bring into being a rank facing the rank of error. They are two ranks facing each other: one rank is the rank of the Prophet; one rank is the rank of the Prophet's enemies.@@CX(Prophethood | society forms into two opposing ranks)@@

What did the Prophet want to do? He wanted to take the people to Heaven — the Heaven of this world and the Heaven after death, both. Because he wants to take the people to Heaven, the people must come with him; if they did not come with him, they do not reach Heaven. Is this matter correct? The Prophet wants to take human beings to the destination of felicity; if they do not come with him, if they do not become his companions and fellow travelers, they do not reach the destination of felicity. Remember this well. Now, between these two ranks there is a person; he looks at the Prophet, he sees that he speaks true words; however much he listens, he sees that the Prophet's word is a good word. On the other hand, he sees that if he comes into the Prophet's rank, he will be compelled to contend, together with that opposing rank, against one another. He has no heart to go into the opposing rank, because he sees that they are going toward Hell; he has no heart to come into the Prophet's rank, because he sees that the Prophet's rank has trouble. What does he do? He comes between these two ranks and chooses a safe, secure, peaceful spot; he pitches a tent there and sits down. What sort of person is this? You tell me.

This man who has sat down in the middle, slumbering between the two ranks on a comfortable bed — will this man reach Heaven or not? It is obvious that he will not; because the Prophet wants to go to Heaven, and those go who set out together with him, while this one has not set out with the Prophet.@@CX(Faith | neutrality between the two ranks does not reach salvation)@@ Whoever is between the two ranks is not with the Prophet; whoever has not joined the Prophet is against the Prophet; whoever is not with Ali(((Ali ibn Abi Talib))) is against Ali; whoever is not with the truth is against the truth.@@CX(Wilayah | whoever is not with the truth is against it)@@ The Quran tells us this too, but the eloquent, clear tongue of the Imam (peace be upon him) — which is much closer to comprehension — states it for us. He says, _"Al-sākitu akhū al-rāḍī"_footnote:s22-4[_"Al-sākitu akhū al-rāḍī"_ ("The silent one is the brother of the one content") — Hadith no. 23; reference: _Biḥār al-Anwār_, the volume of admonitions.] — learn this hadith; the reference for the hadith is _Biḥār al-Anwār_, the volume of admonitions (_mawāʿiẓ_) — _"Al-sākitu akhū al-rāḍī, wa man lam yakun maʿanā kāna ʿalaynā."_ The one who is silent in the face of a false situation is the brother of the one who is content with that situation. They do not even go on to say what the one who is content is; that is obvious — _man raḍiya bi-ʿamali qawmin fa-huwa minhum_: whoever is content with the deed of a community, content with the life of a people, is one of them; they too will take him and tie him to the same manger to which they tie those people. This one who is silent — even though in his heart he is discontented, yet he does not proclaim this discontent — he too is the brother of the one who is content. Then it continues: _"wa man lam yakun maʿanā kāna ʿalaynā"_ — whoever is not with us is against us. Between these two ranks, Islam has not supposed any place.@@CX(Faith | silence before falsehood makes one complicit)@@

Those who, like the companions of ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd(((ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd))) — among them this very gentleman Rabīʿ ibn Khuthaym(((Rabīʿ ibn Khuthaym)))footnote:s22-5[Rabīʿ ibn Khuthaym was an ascetic and devout man, counted among the Successors (_tābiʿīn_). They imagined that the war between Yazdgerd the Third and — I do not know — Heraclius the umpteenth of Rome was such that, if either of them prevailed, it would be of no benefit at all to a person's religion and worldly life! So let a person, seeking ease, conclude a pact of non-aggression, conclude an agreement of non-intervention in the war, and go and sit in some corner. They imagined it was an international war in which several nations are fighting one another over power and government; whichever country can keep itself aside has won. They did not understand that the war between truth and falsehood is an unavoidable war. They did not know that in the war between truth and falsehood, if you were not with the truth, you are with falsehood.@@CX(Faith | the war of truth and falsehood admits no neutrality)@@ To be with falsehood does not necessarily mean that you definitely fight against the truth; rather, even that case in which you do not fight for the truth is also included in the concept of being with falsehood. This they did not understand. Prophethood comes and makes the ranks distinct; it says, "Let those who are with us come."

Why do you not act upon that which you say with your tongue, "I believe in it"? Woe unto me — I am describing my own condition. It is a great sin for a person to say something with the tongue, to declare a belief, but not act upon it.@@CX(Faith | professing a belief without acting on it is a great sin)@@ Why should I say I am a follower of the Prophet of God, while I am a follower of Abū Jahl(((Abū Jahl)))? Why should I say I am after Islam, while I am after polytheism(((shirk)))? Why should I say I am an ʿAlawī (a follower of Ali), while I am of Muʿāwiya(((Muʿāwiya))) and of Abū Jahl?

What was the difference between Ali and Muʿāwiya? By God, today you sketch out Ali and Muʿāwiya in your society. Picture one person of whom everything he says is against ease and indulgence and personal comfort, everything he says lays a burden of responsibility on the shoulder, everything he wants is effort. He is repelled by lying; he is made uneasy by a bribe; he is made uneasy by a gift that gives off the smell of a bribe. On the path of God and for God's sake he is fierce.footnote:s22-8[(n-m-r) angry, leopard-tempered (_mutanammir_: fierce, enraged).] He has a strange leopard-temper on the path of God. On the path of God and the ruling of God he shows mercy to no one; even his own brother, when he comes and asks him for money from the public treasury (_bayt al-māl_)(((bayt al-māl))), he places red-hot iron toward his fist. One such person in society — strict, precise, cautious in the execution of the divine and Islamic rulings and prescribed limits — this is one man. And one man is his exact opposite; he is willing for a person to make merry, willing for a person to live in comfort, willing — whatever you want from him — to give it to you, and he sets only one condition before a person in return, and that condition is that he says, "Do not help Ali, help me; and the help I want from you is not much help either — sometimes praise me, and that is enough." Well, by God, of these two persons, after which one do you go? Which one do you accept in this time and in this segment of history?@@CX(Wilayah | the test is choosing the man of responsibility over the man of comfort)@@ Are you willing to go with him, to be with him, to act upon his command — the one who has trouble, responsibility, movement, effort — to be with him? Are you willing to pass over the one who has money for a person, has rank, has comfort, has title, has influence, has a powerful hand — to pass over him for the sake of this? If you are willing, blessed are you; if you had been alive in Ali's time too, you would have been a Shia of Ali. But if you see that your heart takes flight toward comforts, indulgences, pleasures, monies, ranks, prestige, preening and boasting before this person and that — even if it be outside the path of God — then know that if you had been there, you would have been one of those who, if you were very calculating, would by night, secretly, without having bid farewell to the neighbors, mount your horse, even telling your wife and children, "I await you in Sham (Syria)(((Sham))); yā ʿAlī madad!" — you would go off toward Sham, you would leave Ali alone, just as many of the respected figures of that time left him.

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās(((ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās))), the cousin of the Commander of the Faithful and cousin of the Prophet, the narrator of so much hadith, the commentator of the Quran, a figure respected among Shia(((Shia))) and Sunni(((Sunni))) — did this very thing to Ali, so that the Commander of the Faithful, in _Nahj al-Balāgha_(((Nahj al-Balāgha))), has two letters and two addresses to ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās. And who was ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās, after all? Merely because they narrated some four hadiths from him, on the authority of the Prophet, both Shia and Sunni accept him, all accept him. He was not even a Companion of the Prophet, yet all accept him — and this is a point worth noting. If he had been a Companion of the Prophet and Shia and Sunni accepted him, it would not matter — like Salmān(((Salmān al-Fārisī))), like Abū Dharr(((Abū Dharr))), like ʿAmmār(((ʿAmmār ibn Yāsir))), whom all accept. But he is one of the Successors (_tābiʿīn_)(((tābiʿīn))); he did not properly perceive the time of the Prophet; he was a child when the Prophet passed away. I was studying a bit of history, and I saw that this gentleman ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās was among the companions and entourage of the second Caliph; he was very fond of him too — this one also was mostly in the train of Mr. ʿUmar(((ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb))). For a man to be one of the Successors, not to have perceived the time of the Prophet, and yet for both Shia and Sunni to accept him — _yā lal-ʿajab!_ (how astonishing!) — this must be a very strange man. What is the name for this in Islamic convention — that two hostile groups should both accept one person? This man, while he was the governor and ruler (_ustāndār_) of Basra(((Basra))), took the monies of the public treasury and made his getaway! Where to? Mecca(((Mecca))) — into the safe and secure sanctuary of the Lord. No doubt he took the monies there and gave them in charity! Now, if ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās were here today, what do you suppose he would say about the Commander of the Faithful? He would narrate all the first-hand hadiths on the merit of Ali; he would shed tears at the remembrance of Ali; from having been with Ali he would recount memories. But would you and I — clever and shrewd as we are — accept that he is a Shia? We would say: "Sir, go set this snare for some other bird,footnote:s22-9["Go set this snare for some other bird, / for the ʿAnqā's nest is lofty" (Ḥāfiẓ).] go; if you were a Shia, you ought to have shown yourself at the time of the test." _"ʿInda al-imtiḥān yukramu al-rajulu aw yuhān"_footnote:s22-10[_"ʿInda al-imtiḥān yukramu al-rajulu aw yuhān"_ ("At the time of the test a man is either honored or disgraced") — Hadith no. 19.] — at the time of the test a man is either honored or disgraced.@@CX(Faith | trials reveal the true substance of a person)@@ _"Fī taqallub al-aḥwāl ʿulima jawāhir al-rijāl"_footnote:s22-11[_"Fī taqallub al-aḥwāl ʿulima jawāhir al-rijāl"_ ("In the vicissitudes of circumstances the substances of men become known") — Hadith no. 20.] — in the vicissitudes of circumstances the substances of men become known. If you had been a Shia of Ali, you would not have given Ali so much heart's blood; by your fleeing you would not have wrung out the moaning of Ali so much, such that Ali moaned that way over the departure of ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās: "You were closer to me than all my kinsmen; I had hope in you; I relied upon you. In any case, the commitment of accepting and embracing prophethood is this: walking behind the Prophet, accepting his assignment, acting in the very manner that he wants.@@CX(Prophethood | commitment is to walk behind the Prophet and act as he wills)@@ My time has now grown short for me to explain all the verses; let me explain just this brief portion of the verses for you, so that you may see what the commitment of Islam is.

The Islamic society was newly founded; they had to go, they had to strengthen it, and they had to make that society invulnerable against its enemies; therefore emigration was a decisive condition of the acceptance of faith.@@CX(hijrah | emigration was a decisive condition of faith)@@

Except that there is another ruling alongside it: those very ones who are there — if they happen to fall into war with another group and seek your help, you, of course, must go and aid them, because they are of one mind with you and because they are in a state of war. If a Muslim were engaged in war with a group of unbelievers, it is obligatory upon you — even though that Muslim is not beside you, is not in your homeland, has not emigrated to you — it is obligatory upon you to go and help him. Yes? Except in one case, and that is this: that the one with whom that Muslim is fighting has concluded a peace treaty with you; in that case, helping that Muslim is no longer obligatory. What does this verse make us understand here? First, it makes us understand that helping a Muslim, wherever in the world they may be, is obligatory, even if they have not emigrated.@@CX(Wilayah | aiding a Muslim is obligatory wherever he may be)@@ Second, it says that the Muslim who has not emigrated, who has not migrated to the Islamic society — although of course today, in the world, we do not have an Islamic society in this sense — who has not come to the Islamic society, who has remained in the abode of unbelief (_dār al-kufr_)(((dār al-kufr))), this man, if he fell into war with one or a group of unbelievers, and you have a peace treaty and non-aggression pact with that unbeliever, you have no right to go to the aid of your Muslim brother.
