## Findings

### 1. MEANING-ERROR (minor) — "drowning in armor" vs. "submerged/clad in armor"
- **Source (lines ~281-285):** «خـود را عریان می‌بیند ،دشـمن را تا زیر چشـم و تـا دندان غرق در زره و سالح می‌بیند»
- **English (line 35):** "sees himself as naked and the enemy as **drowning in armor** and weapons up to below the eye and to the teeth"
- **Issue:** غرق در زره here means the enemy is *encased / submerged in / clad up to the eyes in* armor (i.e., heavily armored), not literally "drowning." "Drowning in armor" reads as a near-literal calque that slightly distorts the image — the point is the enemy looks *fully protected*, not *struggling/sinking*.
- **Fix:** "...sees himself as naked and the enemy as encased in armor and weapons up to below the eyes and to the teeth" (or "armored to the teeth").

### 2. MINOR / OPTIONAL — clarity of "up to below the eye and to the teeth"
- Same sentence. تا زیر چشم و تا دندان is the Persian idiom "armed to the teeth." The literal rendering is acceptable for a translation that elsewhere preserves literalism, but "armed/armored to the teeth" would be more natural English. Optional.

### 3. MINOR / OPTIONAL — "Mount Himalaya"
- **English (lines 51):** "even Mount Himalaya" renders کوه هیمالیا literally. The source itself says "Mount Himalaya" (singular, as the speaker said it), so this is *faithful to the source* and arguably should be kept. Flagging only so the editor can decide whether to silently normalize to "the Himalayas"; fidelity does not require it.

### 4. NOTE (not an error) — bracketed clarifications added by translator
- The English adds a few helper glosses in brackets that are not literally in the Persian but are faithful and aid the reader, e.g. "Mecca **[the pilgrimage]**" (line 85) for «مکه را گفته‌اند عمری یک‌بار», and "(Bayt al-Maqdis or Bayt al-Maqdas)" (line 153). These are correct interpretive expansions of the source's elliptical phrasing and do not alter meaning. No change needed.
