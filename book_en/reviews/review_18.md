## Findings

### 1. MINOR (meaning nuance) — "love of mastery / arrogance" Tāghūt clause
**Source (line ~974):** «گاهی آقایی‌طلبی یک آدم طاغوت اوست، تکبر یک انسان طاغوت اوست.»
**English (line 105):** "Sometimes a person's love of mastery is his Tāghūt; the arrogance of a human being is his Tāghūt."
**Assessment:** Faithful. No fix needed — listed only to confirm the full Tāghūt enumeration (self / babbling heart / day-and-night caprice / love-of-mastery / arrogance / external powers) is all present.

### 2. MINOR (meaning) — "گورستان فضیلت‌ها" rendered creatively
**Source (lines 389–396):** «در سرزمین بی‌حاصلِ آن، گورستان فضیلت‌ها، بنا کردند رفتن و نغمۀ توحید سر دادن…»
**English (line 49):** "…and began, in that barren land barren of virtues, to build a cemetery of virtues, and to raise up the melody of Tawhid…"
**Issue:** The Persian here is itself somewhat garbled/elliptical (extraction artifact), and "build a cemetery of virtues" (گورستان فضیلت‌ها بنا کردند) is rendered literally but reads oddly in English; the sense is closer to the barren land *becoming/being made* a graveyard-turned-garden of virtues. **Fix (optional):** consider a light clarifying gloss, e.g. "to make [it] a wellspring of virtues" or footnote the literal phrase. Low priority — literal rendering is defensible.

### 3. MINOR (terminology) — "اعتقادکی بود" diminutive
**Source (line 492):** «البته اعتقادکی بود، تعصب بود، ولی مسائل اجتماعی بالاتر از همه بود.»
**English (line 53):** "Of course, there was a little belief, there was bigotry — but the social considerations were above all else."
**Assessment:** "a little belief" correctly captures the diminutive اعتقادکی. Accurate; no fix.

### 4. MINOR (idiom) — "شستِ دوست و دشمن خبردار می‌شود"
**Source (line 382):** «شستِ دوست و دشمن خبردار می‌شود که قضیه چه خبر است.»
**English (line 47):** "the thumbs of friend and foe alike are alerted as to what is afoot" + footnote [^6].
**Assessment:** Idiom correctly rendered with footnote explaining the proverb. Accurate.

### 5. MINOR (completeness check) — Aʿrāf 7:71 "رجس و غضب" doublet
**Source (lines ~1376–1384):** «رِجس و غضب… پلیدی‌ای در وجود خودتان و خشمی از ناحیۀ پروردگارتان شما را فراگرفت؛ بدبخت هستید.»
**English (line 165):** "a defilement within your own beings, and a wrath from the side of your Lord has overtaken you; you are wretched."
**Assessment:** Complete and faithful, including "بدبخت هستید" → "you are wretched."
