## Findings

### 1. [MINOR — meaning/nuance] "dimokrāt-e tārikh" rendered as "Democritus of history"
- **Source (lines 48–50):** «مادیون روزگار ما ـ کار به دیموکرات تاریخ نداریم، ذیمقراطیس یا فالن دانشمند مادی ملحدِ دیگر که ده قرن پیش...»
- **English (line 9):** "the materialists of our age — and we are not concerned here with Democritus of history, that other materialist atheist of ten centuries ago, Democritus or some such scholar..."
- **Note:** The Persian says "ten centuries ago" (ده قرن پیش) for the historical materialist, then a few lines later (line 63) says «بیست قرن پیش، سی قرن پیش» (twenty/thirty centuries ago). The English correctly carries both ("ten centuries of ago" at line 9, "twenty centuries, thirty centuries ago" also at line 9). This is faithful. The doubled "Democritus ... Democritus" mirrors the source's «دیموکرات ... ذیمقراطیس» (two transliterations of the same name), so this is acceptable, though a reader may find it slightly redundant. **Fix (optional):** none required; could add a translator note that Dimokrāt and Zimoqrāṭis are the same figure, but footnote 2 already clarifies this.

### 2. [MINOR — meaning] "ده قرن پیش" vs the historical figure's actual dating
- This is the author's own rhetorical looseness (Democritus lived ~24 centuries before 1974, not 10/20/30), faithfully preserved. Not a translation error. No fix.
