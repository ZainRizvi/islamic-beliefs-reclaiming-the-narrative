## Findings

### 1. [MINOR — meaning error] "غرائز انسانی و بشری" rendered "human and animal instincts"
- **Source (L603):** «می‌آید حواس ظاهره را، غرائز انسانی و بشری را، نیروی خرد و اندیشۀ آدمی را تقویت می‌کند»
- **English (L55):** "it comes to strengthen the outward senses, **the human and animal instincts**, and the force of the intellect and thought of man"
- **Issue:** بشری means *human / of mankind / mortal*, not *animal*. The phrase «انسانی و بشری» is a near-synonymous pairing ("human and mankind's"). Rendering بشری as "animal" introduces a concept (animality) not in the source.
- **Fix:** "the human and mortal instincts" (or simply "the human instincts," or "the instincts of man"). Note: the same pairing earlier (L190–192 «غرائز انسان») is handled fine as "the instincts of man," so this is an isolated slip.

### 2. [TRIVIAL — translator addition, not an error] Added [^zakariyya] footnote
- **Source (L680–684):** the tree-sawing / Yaḥyā passage has **no** footnote in the Persian.
- **English (L71, L159):** translator added an explanatory [^zakariyya] footnote about Zakariyyā being sawed inside a tree.
- **Assessment:** This is a helpful editorial gloss, not a fidelity violation. No action required; flagged only for transparency. (Likewise [^taleqani] expands the bare source footnote "a tafsīr by Ayatollah Ṭāleqānī" with the title *Partuvī az Qurʾān* — also acceptable.)
