## Minor nuance notes (optional; not summarization, not meaning-altering)

### N1 — "translations" vs. "meanings of the words" (LOW)
- **Source** (PR 673–684): «…ترجمه‌ها را درست دقت کنید… هر کلمه‌ای را که من … تکیه کردم … فوراً به معانی کلمات برگردید.»
- **EN**: "Pay close attention to the translations… I have laid stress on every word I emphasized… you may immediately return to the meanings of the words."
- **Note**: Faithful and complete. The OCR splits this sentence awkwardly (the "(ترجمه‌ها…" fragment appears earlier near PR 647), but the English correctly reassembles it. No action needed; flagged only so a later editor doesn't "fix" what looks like a displaced clause.

### N2 — «معظم‌له» rendered "the author" (LOW)
- **Source** (PR 991–992): «در کتابچۀ … که معظم‌له در پایان جلسات تنظیم کرده‌اند…»
- **EN** (line 125): "…which the author arranged at the conclusion of the sessions…"
- **Note**: معظم‌له is an honorific ("His Eminence," referring to the speaker, i.e. Khamenei). "The author" is an acceptable functional rendering and reads naturally in English; if the project prefers to preserve the honorific register elsewhere, consider "which His Eminence arranged." Not an error — purely a stylistic/register choice.
