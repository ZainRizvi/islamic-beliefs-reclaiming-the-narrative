## Minor observations (not errors; no action required)

1. **¶208 / src 1200–1214 (self-correction aside).** Source: "نمی‌دانم در این ترجمه‌ای که اینجا کردم، من چه انتخاب کردم برای «شدید القوی»… یکی از این کاغذها را لطف کنید. بله، که آن بسی نیرومند به او آموخته است." EN: "(I do not know what I chose in this translation that I made here, for 'one mighty in power'… Hand me one of these papers, please. Yes — 'that one of great might has taught him.')" — Faithful, including the live correction. No issue.

2. **Verse-reference granularity.** The translator sometimes splits a single source recitation into multiple EN quote blocks with per-verse references (e.g., al-Najm 53:8–10 grouped at ¶232). This is an editorial enhancement that *adds* precision; it neither drops nor alters source content.

3. **"دو صد من بار، بر روی صد من استخوان"** rendered "two hundred maunds of burden may rest upon a hundred maunds of the prophet's bone" with proverb footnote [^10]. The source proverb in the footnote is "دو صد من استخوان باید که صد من بار بردارد" (two hundred of bone to carry one hundred of burden); the footnote renders it correctly. The body text's "two hundred maunds of burden on a hundred maunds of bone" matches the *body* of the source (src 248–249: "این دو صد من بار، بر روی صد من استخوان"), which inverts the proverb's ratio — but this inversion is **in the source itself**, faithfully reproduced. Not a translation error.
